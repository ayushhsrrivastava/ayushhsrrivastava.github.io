01. The "Cold Start" Brief

I was brought into this project during a critical delay period. Handed over without a formal Knowledge Transfer (KT), I inherited a stalled "Site Tunneling" initiative between a major fashion house's SAP system and a leading e-commerce marketplace.

The objective was to enable real-time inventory updates and data interchange between the two massive ecosystems. My first priority was rapid discovery—deciphering the project status via Jira backlogs and immediate technical audits.

Fig 1: Infrastructure Tunneling & Network Mapping Audit

02. Technical Detective Work

Environment Mismatch

Discovered that the tunneling was failing due to a legacy configuration. The system was still attempting to route traffic through Pre-Production IPs instead of the finalized Production environment.

TTL & Latency Optimization

Identified a critical configuration error where ping intervals were set to 36,000ms. I recalibrated this to 3,600ms, immediately stabilizing the data interchange loop.

03. The Rescue Strategy

To bypass the lack of documentation, I implemented a 24-hour turnaround strategy:

  • 01. War Room Mobilization: Hosted a joint technical session with cross-functional stakeholders to map the technical architecture from scratch and identify hidden blockers.
  • 02. Confluence Centricity: Created a single source of truth by documenting every technical variable, IP, and stakeholder dependency that was previously unrecorded.
  • 03. Root Cause Closure: Spearheaded the step-by-step audit of the "Site Tunneling" setup, correcting environment headers and whitelisting protocols in a single afternoon.
